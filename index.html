The webpage serves as reflections of 3 major projects about Data for ENGL 3844 class

The projects of the Writing and Digital Media class cover how we perceive the use of data. Data Collection: Soonyoung's Mood Tracking on usage of YouTube

The theme of my data collection is to see how my mood spectrum can be influenced by the genre of videos on YouTube. The following explanation of different columns will make you understand clear about the spreadsheet.

  • Column A: Date - This is the date with year when I watched the video on YouTube. I used different color on different day.
  • Column B: Timestamp - This indicates the exact time when I started to watch the video. The time is recorded as chronological order (AM/PM was put).
  • Column C: Genere - I typed the first initials of the genres that I spend time on watching. Korean Music = km, Classical Music = cm, Self-Improvement Lectures = sl, Beauty-Tutorial Videos= bv, Coding Lectures = cl, Workout Videos = wv, Random Videos = rv
  • Column D: Link - This is the title with the link. If you open the spreadsheet, you will be able to naviage the videos that I watched.
  • Column E: Device - The column indicates which device I used when I watch or listen to YouTube. Laptop = l & Cellphone = c
  • Column F: Mood - This is state of mind when watching or listening to the videos on YouTube.
  • Column G: Intensity - This indicates how strong I was feeling the specific mood. The intensity is 1 to 5.
  • Column H: Engagement - This indicates the engagement whether I liked,disliked or indifferent to the videos I watched on YouTube.

    • Data Visualization: Visualization on the Data collection:Soonyoung's Mood Tracking on usage of YouTube

    This project is visualization based on using the results of the data collection project. The entire graph is stacked bar chart and the project has several visual attributes. The following list is to help you understand the project.

    1. The title (Soonyoung's YouTube MoodTrack) with two different faces(emotions) of mine give you ideas what it's about the project.
    2. Different use of color with its gradiation from 1 to 5 is different mood and its intensity.
    3. The first row of the chart indicates it is the genre of videos that I watched.
    4. I drew the chart in order of frequency of listening to the genre. So, I only listened to classical music 3 times total while I watched coding lecture 13 times during a week of period.
    5. The color of bargraph in vertical way represents from passive mood (sleepy,relaxed,sad) to more active mood (pleasant, focused, intense).
    6. However, there are some neutral emotions such as satisfaction/unsatisfaction that I put between passive mood and active mood.
    The conclusion that I made from this projet, the classical music, workout videos, self improvement lectures, and coding lectures tend to result positive mood while Korean music, beauty-tip videos or random videos on YouTube tend to result more various emotions with some negative mood like sad and unpleasant feelings.

    Explained Video: Content Moderation

    The video is cut to 30 seconds due to the limited size rules. The original video can be shared upon a request via email at soon95@vt.edu

    The group project of Explained Video was developed by Anthony Farina, Soonyoung Kim, and Heather Trobridge.

    The topic of the video is about Content Moderation, and the video on the left is the final project. The video is separated by three scenes in order of explanation made by Anthony, Soonyoung, and Heather.

    1. The scene 1 covers the definition and social media's content moderation platforms.
    2. The scene 2 covers the positive and negative outcomes of content moderation, and Human content moderation worker's responsibilities and difficulties.
    3. The scene 3 covers the positive and negative aspects of Algorithm content moderation, and the ethics of content moderation.

    The associated research of the project led me to ponder how I've used the social media data without fear of content moderation. Thinking of some people or even machine based program will always watch me how I use digital data make me feel quite insecure freely enjoying social media.
